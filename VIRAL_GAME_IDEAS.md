## Market Research Summary (March 2026)

### Browser Games Resurgence
Browser games are back in a big way. The market is projected to reach $9.07B by 2030 (3.1% CAGR). Modern HTML5, WebGL, and WebAssembly have closed the quality gap with native apps. Social media embedding (TikTok, LinkedIn, Discord) and zero-friction access are the primary growth drivers.

### What Went Viral in 2024-2025
- **NYT Games dominance**: Wordle (10M+ daily), Connections (second most-played NYT game), Strands (themed word search), and the Mini Crossword form a daily ritual ecosystem. The key insight: daily cadence + emoji-grid sharing + no-spoiler culture = viral loops.
- **LinkedIn Games**: Pinpoint, Tango (binary grid logic), Queens, Crossclimb, and Zip brought daily puzzles to a professional audience. Proved puzzles work in ANY social context.
- **Block Blast**: A simple drag-and-drop block puzzle became one of the most downloaded games globally in 2025. No timers, no stress -- pure spatial satisfaction. Proves that relaxing puzzles beat frantic action for mass market.
- **Tile/Match-3 evolution**: Stack elimination puzzles (inspired by "Sheep A Sheep" on WeChat) spawned 100+ clones. Tile Club hit 10M downloads.
- **Human Benchmark tests**: Reaction time, chimp test, number memory, and aim trainers went viral on TikTok. "Am I smarter than a chimp?" is irresistible content.
- **Monkeytype/TypeRacer**: Competitive typing tests became social content. WPM scores are the new IQ flex.
- **Meme-aesthetic casual games**: "Italian Brainrot" and Doge-themed games drove mass downloads. Absurd visuals + viral phrases = downloads.
- **Incremental/idle games**: Cookie Clicker and Universal Paperclips remain evergreen. The "just one more upgrade" loop is timeless.

### Viral Mechanics That Work
1. **Emoji grid results** (Wordle model) -- spoiler-free sharing that creates FOMO
2. **Daily seeds** -- everyone plays the same puzzle, enabling comparison
3. **Challenge links** -- encoded seeds in URLs for 1v1 competition
4. **Identity/ability scores** -- "what's YOUR score?" drives self-expression sharing
5. **Beautiful failure states** -- when losing produces art (generative visuals, splatter patterns)
6. **Cognitive dissonance** -- doing familiar things wrong (Tetris Betrayal, reverse Minesweeper)
7. **Physical comedy** -- ragdoll physics, dual-control chaos (great for video content)
8. **Time-of-day mechanics** -- real-world clock integration creates "3AM challenge" content
9. **Streak tracking** -- daily streaks create habit and fear of losing progress
10. **Percentile ranking** -- "Top 3% globally" is more shareable than raw scores

## Viral Design Principles (apply to every game)

- **Zero friction**: Click link -> playing in <3 seconds. No signup, no download.
- **Sessions <60s**: Fits TikTok-era attention. "One more try" loops.
- **Shareable results**: Emoji grids, screenshots, challenge links. Show off without spoiling.
- **Daily cadence OR ultra-short retry**: Wordle model (scarcity + habit) or Block Blast model (relaxing infinite play).
- **Simple to learn, hard to master**: Understandable in 5 seconds.
- **Emotional spikes**: Surprise, frustration, awe, laughter -> sharing.

### New Ideas -- Ranked

#### 1. CHIMP
**Type:** Cognitive test / arcade
**Mechanic:** Recreation of the famous Chimp Memory Test from Human Benchmark. Numbers appear briefly on a grid, then hide behind tiles. Tap them in ascending order. Each level adds one more number and shortens display time. How far past the chimp threshold (level 9) can you go?
**Why viral:** "Are you smarter than a chimpanzee?" is irresistible bait. TikTok is flooded with chimp test content. Failure at level 7 while chimps clear 9 is humiliating and shareable.
**Sharing:** `Level 12 | Top 4% | Better than 97% of chimps`
**Build difficulty:** 1/5

#### 2. TYPEBLITZ
**Type:** Speed challenge / competitive
**Mechanic:** Monkeytype-style typing speed test with neon aesthetic. 15/30/60 second modes. Random words or quotes. WPM score + accuracy. Challenge links with same word set. Live ghost cursor showing friend's replay.
**Why viral:** WPM is the new IQ score on social media. Direct competition via challenge links. "Beat my 127 WPM" is an irresistible dare.
**Sharing:** `TYPEBLITZ | 127 WPM | 98.4% accuracy | Top 6%`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 3. POUR
**Type:** Physics / fluid sim
**Mechanic:** Tilt device (or use keys) to pour colored particle-fluid into target containers. Obstacles redirect your flow. Overfill creates beautiful splatter art. 30 levels of increasing complexity. Every failure becomes a unique abstract painting.
**Why viral:** Fluid physics is inherently satisfying to watch. Splatter patterns from failures are screenshot-worthy generative art. ASMR-adjacent content for short-form video.
**Sharing:** Screenshots of splatter art + level reached + accuracy %
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 4. FREEFALL
**Type:** Arcade / ragdoll physics
**Mechanic:** Endless falling through an obstacle course. You control the WIND direction, not the ragdoll character. Tilt, nudge, and blast your floppy body through gaps, around spikes, and through rings. Every near-miss is cinematic. Score = depth reached.
**Why viral:** Ragdoll physics = inherently hilarious. Gap between player intention and ragdoll result = comedy gold. Perfect short-form video content. Watching someone scream at their floppy character is universal entertainment.
**Sharing:** Depth + replay clips of spectacular wipeouts
**Build difficulty:** 3/5

#### 5. ECHOFIELD
**Type:** Arcade / exploration
**Mechanic:** Navigate a pitch-black space. Tap to emit a sonar pulse that briefly reveals walls and obstacles. Pulses persist as fading echoes. Build a mental map from layered echoes. Collect nodes. Avoid creatures that hunt by YOUR sound.
**Why viral:** The sonar visual effect (expanding rings revealing geometry) is stunning. Creates tension -- every pulse reveals but also attracts danger. Beautiful screenshots of layered echo rings.
**Sharing:** Nodes collected + map coverage % + pulse efficiency
**Build difficulty:** 3/5

#### 6. ORBIT
**Type:** Physics / precision arcade
**Mechanic:** Planets orbit a central star. Tap to launch satellites that must enter stable orbit around each planet. Gravity simulation means trajectories curve. Thread satellites between gravitational fields. Beautiful orbital trails.
**Why viral:** Orbital mechanics are mesmerizing to watch. Near-misses create dramatic arcs. Multi-planet orbital captures feel like genius moments. Trail visualizations are art.
**Sharing:** Planets captured + orbital screenshot + efficiency rating
**Build difficulty:** 3/5

#### 7. CONDUCTOR
**Type:** Rhythm / arcade
**Mechanic:** Musical notes approach from 4 directions on converging tracks. Swipe to redirect them into the correct instrument zones. Tempo increases. Missed notes create dissonance. Perfect runs build beautiful layered music. Wrong redirects create cacophony.
**Why viral:** Creates actual music through gameplay. Perfect runs sound amazing and are shareable as audio clips. The contrast between a perfect run and a messy one is dramatic and funny.
**Sharing:** Score + streak + "listen to my run" audio link
**Build difficulty:** 3/5

#### 8. MIRRORLOCK
**Type:** Puzzle / arcade
**Mechanic:** Control two characters on a diagonally-split screen. They mirror each other's movements (one moves left, the other moves right). Navigate both through separate obstacle courses simultaneously. What helps one often hurts the other.
**Why viral:** Brain-breaking spatial reasoning. "My left brain fought my right brain" moments. Watching someone struggle with mirrored controls is entertaining content.
**Sharing:** Levels cleared + death count + time
**Build difficulty:** 3/5

#### 9. BLOOMFIELD
**Type:** Creative / generative art
**Mechanic:** Tap to plant neon seeds on a dark field. Each grows into a procedural glowing flower with unique petal patterns. Nearby plants bend toward each other. Limited seeds per round. Score based on coverage + beauty heuristic (symmetry, variety, spacing).
**Why viral:** Every garden is unique neon art. Screenshots look like prints worth framing. "Look at MY garden" sharing is powerful. Meditative gameplay contrasts with competitive scoring.
**Sharing:** Garden screenshot + beauty score + seed efficiency
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 10. VETO
**Type:** Social / bracket challenge
**Mechanic:** 16-item bracket (movies, foods, songs, etc.). Send bracket link to friend. Both independently eliminate items to a winner. One VETO power each. Reveal: see where you agreed, disagreed, and your compatibility score. New theme daily.
**Why viral:** "Do we have the same taste?" is a universal social hook. Bracket debates are endlessly engaging. Daily themes keep it fresh. Compatibility % is the ultimate shareable stat.
**Sharing:** `VETO | Pizza vs Tacos bracket | 73% match with @friend`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 11. SPACE TRAITORS
**Type:** Arcade / deduction
**Mechanic:** Space Invaders but some of your ally ships at the bottom are traitors shooting at you. Identify and destroy traitors without killing loyal allies. Traitor kill: +500. Ally kill: lose firepower. Paranoia increases as the game progresses.
**Why viral:** Paranoia in a single-player game. "Was it Ship 3?!" debates are clip-worthy. Combines reflex action with deductive reasoning.
**Sharing:** Score + traitors caught + innocents killed (guilt metric)
**Build difficulty:** 3/5

#### 12. FROGGER FOREVER
**Type:** Arcade / time-clone
**Mechanic:** Endless Frogger. Every 30 seconds, a clone frog spawns replaying your movements from 30s ago. If ANY frog dies, game over. By minute 3, you have 6 frogs following your historical paths through traffic.
**Why viral:** Watching the frog chain navigate traffic is hypnotic. "Time-paradox planning" is a unique cognitive challenge. The moment your past self gets hit by a car you dodged is devastating.
**Sharing:** Survival time + max clone count + replay
**Build difficulty:** 4/5

### New Ideas -- Ranked

#### 1. SLITHERLINK
**Type:** Logic puzzle (Nikoli classic)
**Mechanic:** Grid of dots with numbers in some cells. Draw a single continuous loop along the grid edges so that each numbered cell has exactly that many edges of the loop around it. No branches, no crossings -- one perfect loop.
**Why viral:** Elegant, satisfying "aha" moments. The loop-closing moment is deeply gratifying. Daily puzzles with emoji-grid sharing (loop shape as visual). Huge existing fanbase from Nikoli.
**Sharing:** `SLITHERLINK #42 | 5x5 | 2:14 | No hints`
**Build difficulty:** 3/5

#### 2. LIGHTUP (Akari)
**Type:** Logic puzzle (Nikoli classic)
**Mechanic:** Place light bulbs on a grid so every white cell is illuminated. Bulbs shine horizontally and vertically until blocked by a wall. No bulb may illuminate another bulb. Numbered walls indicate exactly how many bulbs must be adjacent.
**Why viral:** Visual satisfaction of "lighting up" the entire grid. Clear, intuitive rules. Mistakes are immediately visible (overlapping light = red glow). Great difficulty curve from 5x5 to 14x14.
**Sharing:** `LIGHTUP #17 | 10x10 | 3:47 | Perfect`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 3. TANGO
**Type:** Binary logic puzzle (LinkedIn-style)
**Mechanic:** Fill a grid so each row and column contains equal numbers of two symbols (suns and moons / 0s and 1s). No three consecutive same symbols in a row or column. Some cells are pre-filled. Pure logic, no guessing.
**Why viral:** LinkedIn's Tango proved this format works for mainstream audiences. Simple rules, deeply satisfying deduction chains. Daily puzzle with streak tracking. Accessible to non-puzzle-enthusiasts.
**Sharing:** `TANGO #88 | 8x8 | 1:32 | Streak: 14 days`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 4. STAR BATTLE
**Type:** Logic puzzle (competition classic)
**Mechanic:** Place stars in a grid divided into regions. Each row, column, and region must contain exactly the specified number of stars. Stars cannot touch each other, even diagonally. Start with 1-star puzzles, graduate to 2-star.
**Why viral:** Used in the World Puzzle Championship -- prestigious association. Clean, visual puzzle with dramatic difficulty scaling. The constraint propagation creates satisfying chain deductions. LinkedIn's Queens is a simpler variant -- this is the "hard mode."
**Sharing:** `STAR BATTLE #31 | 10x10 / 2-star | 4:55`
**Build difficulty:** 3/5

#### 5. MASYU
**Type:** Loop puzzle (Nikoli classic)
**Mechanic:** Draw a single continuous loop through a grid. The loop must pass through every circle. At white circles, the loop goes straight through but must turn in at least one adjacent cell. At black circles, the loop must turn 90 degrees and go straight in both adjacent cells.
**Why viral:** Beautiful visual result (elegant loop through pearls). The black/white pearl rules create cascading logic. Puzzle community favorite with dedicated online following. Distinctive, unlike anything mainstream.
**Sharing:** `MASYU #56 | 10x10 | 3:22 | Clean solve`
**Build difficulty:** 3/5

#### 6. TENTS
**Type:** Logic puzzle (classic)
**Mechanic:** Place tents on a grid next to trees (each tree gets exactly one tent). Tents cannot touch each other, even diagonally. Row and column numbers tell you how many tents belong in each. Pure deduction from the number constraints.
**Why viral:** Charming camping theme is more approachable than abstract puzzles. Numbers on edges give it a Minesweeper-like feel. "Pairing" tents to trees adds a matching layer that feels fresh. Great for beginners graduating from Sudoku.
**Sharing:** `TENTS #23 | 8x8 | 2:08 | All paired`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 7. SKYSCRAPERS
**Type:** Latin square logic puzzle
**Mechanic:** Fill an NxN grid with numbers 1-N (each row/column has each number once, like Sudoku). Clues around the edges tell you how many "skyscrapers" are visible from that direction (taller buildings block shorter ones behind them). Pure deduction.
**Why viral:** 3D reasoning in a 2D puzzle is mind-bending. The "visibility" mechanic is intuitive (everyone understands buildings blocking views). Satisfying "I can see it!" moments. Great difficulty scaling from 4x4 to 7x7.
**Sharing:** `SKYSCRAPERS #15 | 6x6 | 2:45 | No errors`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 8. HITORI
**Type:** Elimination logic puzzle (Nikoli)
**Mechanic:** Grid pre-filled with numbers. Shade cells so that no number appears more than once in any row or column. Shaded cells cannot be adjacent. All unshaded cells must form one connected group. Elimination logic at its purest.
**Why viral:** Satisfying "elimination" mechanic (crossing out duplicates). Connected-group constraint creates surprising deductions. Compact puzzles (5x5 to 9x9) fit short sessions. Unique mechanic not seen in mainstream apps.
**Sharing:** `HITORI #44 | 7x7 | 1:55 | Streak: 7`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 9. FILLOMINO
**Type:** Region-building puzzle (Nikoli)
**Mechanic:** Fill a grid with numbers. Groups of identical adjacent numbers must form a connected polyomino of that size (e.g., three 3s form a tromino). Some numbers are given as seeds. No two polyominoes of the same size may share an edge.
**Why viral:** Creative territory-building feel. The constraint that same-size regions cannot touch creates satisfying spatial reasoning. Emergent from simple rules. Appeals to both math and spatial thinkers.
**Sharing:** `FILLOMINO #33 | 7x7 | 3:10`
**Build difficulty:** 3/5

#### 10. THERMOMETERS
**Type:** Fill puzzle (competition classic)
**Mechanic:** Grid contains thermometer shapes. Fill them from the bulb end up to a certain level. Row and column numbers indicate how many filled cells in each. Logic determines exactly how much to fill each thermometer. No guessing needed.
**Why viral:** Unique visual metaphor (mercury rising). Very intuitive rules -- everyone understands thermometers. The "how much to fill" decision tree creates clean deduction chains. Underrepresented in apps despite being a competition staple.
**Sharing:** `THERMOMETERS #19 | 8x8 | 2:30 | Perfect`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 11. KENKEN
**Type:** Math logic puzzle (educational)
**Mechanic:** Fill an NxN grid with numbers 1-N (Latin square rules). Grid is divided into cages with a target number and operation (+, -, x, /). Numbers in each cage must produce the target using the operation. Invented by a Japanese math teacher.
**Why viral:** "Sudoku meets arithmetic" pitch is instantly clear. Educational angle gets teacher/parent sharing. Difficulty scales beautifully. Used in classrooms worldwide. Operation variety keeps it fresh.
**Sharing:** `KENKEN #27 | 6x6 | Hard | 4:12`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 12. FUTOSHIKI
**Type:** Inequality logic puzzle
**Mechanic:** Fill an NxN grid with numbers 1-N (Latin square). Some cells have inequality signs (< or >) between them that must be satisfied. Combine Sudoku-style elimination with ordering constraints.
**Why viral:** Simple twist on Sudoku that feels fresh. The inequality arrows create visual flow through the puzzle. Quick to learn for anyone who knows Sudoku. Great bite-sized daily puzzles.
**Sharing:** `FUTOSHIKI #51 | 6x6 | 1:48 | Streak: 21`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 13. SHIKAKU
**Type:** Rectangle partition puzzle (Nikoli)
**Mechanic:** Divide a grid into rectangles. Each rectangle must contain exactly one number, and that number equals the rectangle's area. Every cell must belong to exactly one rectangle. No overlaps, no gaps.
**Why viral:** Satisfying geometric partitioning. The number-as-area rule is instantly graspable. Visual result is a clean mosaic of rectangles. Fast puzzles (3-5 minutes) fit mobile sessions.
**Sharing:** `SHIKAKU #38 | 10x10 | 2:15`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

#### 14. NUMBERLINK
**Type:** Path-drawing puzzle (Nikoli)
**Mechanic:** Grid with pairs of identical numbers. Draw paths connecting each pair. Paths cannot cross, branch, or share cells. Every cell must be used. Find the unique way to connect all pairs while filling the entire grid.
**Why viral:** Drawing paths is tactile and satisfying on touchscreens. "Fill every cell" constraint makes it surprisingly tricky. Clean visual result of colored paths. Popular in puzzle competitions.
**Sharing:** `NUMBERLINK #29 | 7x7 | 5 pairs | 1:40`
**Build difficulty:** 3/5

**Category:** NEON MIND (logic) + NEON GRIND (speed)
**Mechanic:** 16 words. Sort them into 4 groups of 4 by finding the hidden connections. 4 guesses allowed. Color-coded difficulty (green = easy, purple = tricky). Same puzzle daily for everyone. Neon Arcade's take on the viral NYT format.
**Why viral:** Proven viral format with 10M+ daily NYT players. Our neon version adds aesthetic differentiation. Daily cadence + emoji grid sharing + "did you get purple?" discussions. The format works because it rewards lateral thinking.
**Sharing:** `CONNECTIONS #142 | [emoji grid] | 2/4 guesses | Streak: 8`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

### 4. DAILY LADDER
**Type:** Word ladder / daily challenge
**Category:** NEON MIND (word logic) + NEON GRIND (speed)
**Mechanic:** Transform one word into another by changing one letter at a time, where each step must be a valid word. E.g., COLD -> CORD -> CARD -> WARD -> WARM. Fewest steps wins. Daily puzzle with global leaderboard. Timed mode for speed demons.
**Why viral:** Classic word puzzle beloved by linguists. "I did it in 4 steps, you needed 7" is competitive gold. LinkedIn's Crossclimb proved word ladders work for mainstream. Daily format creates habit.
**Sharing:** `DAILY LADDER | COLD -> WARM | 4 steps | Optimal! | Streak: 11`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

### 5. MIMIC
**Type:** Social prediction game
**Category:** NEON ARCADE (social) + NEON MIND (deduction)
**Mechanic:** Answer 10 preference questions ("beach or mountains?", "cats or dogs?", "morning or night?"). Send link to friend. They answer AS YOU -- predicting your choices. Reveal how well they know you. Mutual scoring.
**Why viral:** "How well do you ACTUALLY know me?" is the most powerful social hook. Deeply personal, creates relationship conversations. Mutual play means guaranteed second player. Wrong guesses are funnier than right ones.
**Sharing:** `MIMIC | @friend knows me 70% | They thought I prefer cats (wrong!)`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

### 6. WAVELENGTH
**Type:** Spectrum guessing game
**Category:** NEON ARCADE (social) + NEON MIND (judgment)
**Mechanic:** A spectrum between two opposites (e.g., "Hot -- Cold"). A hidden target sits somewhere on the spectrum. One player gives a clue (e.g., "coffee"). Others guess where on the spectrum the clue points. Closer = more points. Daily mode: everyone rates the same clues.
**Why viral:** Debates about where things sit on spectrums are endlessly engaging. "Is pizza a 7/10 on healthy-unhealthy? FIGHT ME." Creates arguments that drive engagement. Boardgame hit translated perfectly to digital.
**Sharing:** `WAVELENGTH | Score: 84/100 | Best guess: "lava" for Hot-Cold`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

### 7. POLYGLOT
**Type:** Language identification speed game
**Category:** NEON GRIND (speed) + NEON MIND (knowledge)
**Mechanic:** Short text snippets appear in different languages. Identify the language as fast as possible from multiple choice. 30 rounds. Starts with obvious ones (English, Chinese, Arabic), gets obscure (Welsh, Basque, Tagalog). Score = speed x accuracy.
**Why viral:** Language knowledge as identity. "I can identify 28 languages" is impressive. Polyglots flex, monolinguals are humbled. Educational and genuinely interesting -- people learn languages they didn't know existed.
**Sharing:** `POLYGLOT | 26/30 correct | Avg: 2.1s | Stumped by: Maltese`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5

### 8. TIMELINE
**Type:** History ordering game
**Category:** NEON GRIND (speed) + NEON MIND (knowledge)
**Mechanic:** Events/inventions/discoveries appear. Place them on a timeline in correct chronological order. Each correct placement earns points. Wrong placement = strike. Three strikes = game over. Daily themed rounds (science, music, sports, etc.).
**Why viral:** History knowledge varies wildly -- arguments guaranteed. "The printing press was BEFORE the compass?!" surprise moments. Daily themes keep it fresh. Educational angle gets sharing from teachers/parents.
**Sharing:** `TIMELINE | Science | 18 placed | Best streak: 12 | "DNA before Moon landing? Obviously."`
**Build difficulty:** 2/5
